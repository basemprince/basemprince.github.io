    AUTONOMOUS TARGET SHOOTING VEHICLE

    • The main objective of the project was to design and manufacture an autonomous vehicle powered by Arduino that can navigate a course and fire projectiles at targets
    • Using knowledge gained in computer aided design, along with background knowledge of design, an autonomous Arduino powered car was able to be produced
    • The vehicle and all of its external components were designed using Solidworks. The additional components required to meet the objective of launching a projectile to a target from the track were manufactured using rapid prototyping (3D printing), laser cutting, and CNC machining
    • The track was designed such that the vehicle required an array of line sensors and bump sensors to successfully navigate it
    • Navigation was based around skid steering, utilizing line sensors mounted to the front of the vehicle and bump sensors on the sides of the vehicle
    • In order to hit the targets, a spring powered rack and pinion launcher was designed, using a continuous hobby servo (for pulling back the rack) and one position control hobby servo (for a rotary magazine/carousel to load BB’s)
    • The launcher was placed on a moveable platform to adjust trajectory. A gear reduction was used to maintain torque when pulling the firing piston (rack) back. The loading mechanism utilized the position control servo to rotate carousel magazine, dropping one BB into the chamber at a time. The magazine was designed with a capacity of six BB’s and to automatically reset itself
    • In order to detect the brass rods on the track indicating launch positions, the bump sensors were mounted on the side of the vehicle using 3D printed brackets

    SLOT DRAIN TABLESS DESIGN

    • All slot drains have welded tabs in their slots to maintain the slot width and shape during the transportaion and installation process
    • The tabs could be later removed by the clinet, however, it could be a difficult task to accomplish
    • Tabs create harboring points, which could be unsanitary. It also creates challenges during the cleaning process
    • To eleminate the need for the tabs, rib like structures were designed and tested using Inventor stress analysis
    • Various configurations of the ribs were tested using Inventor's stress anlaysis with the help of the parametric table
    • The parametric table allows for testing multiple constraints simultainously to optimize for a specific target
    • The target was to only allow for a specific amount of displacement at the neck of the slot drain, with the least amount of material required
    • Once the chosen constraints were given lower and upper limits, the stress analysis study checked every possible combination of the constraints values to find the best solution
    • To decrease the material required to manufacture the ribs, the frame generator feature of Inventor was used. This feature removes material from the ribs that do not contribute to the function of the model as they do not experince stress
    • The best rib design was then used in another simulation to find the needed amount of ribs to install on a single slot drain run
    • The ribs were then produced and welded on a slot drain for real life testing

    PERCEPTION SENSOR FUSION

    • As a member of the Driver-less Formula team I dealt with fusing the input from the camera and lidar sensors
    • The hardware is a Zed stereo camera and a Velodyne VLP-16 with an Nvidia Xavier as a GPU. the Driver-less pipeline uses Robot Operating System (ROS)
    • The Zed camera input is fed into YOLO (You Only Look Once), which is a real-time object detection CNN
    • The YOLO algorithm detects the cones and outputs the color of the cone and the bounding box points locations
    • The point cloud output from the Lidar was used to get the correct distance to the cones and had to be transformed to the camera image frame
    • To compute the transformation, the intrensic and the extrensic matrices had to be calculated first
    • The intrensic and the extrensic matrices were calculated using Matlab's camera calibration tool box
    • After the point cloud was transformed to camera frame, the bounding boxes from Yolo were used to filter out the transformed point cloud
    • The output now includes the location, distance and color of all cones along with a time stamp and the covariance matrices
    • The lookup output is later fed to the next step in the pipeline, which is SLAM
    • Designed and 3d printed a bracket holder to connect the lidar and camera together for consistent locating during calibration procedures

    ROBOT MISSION PLANNER SIMULATION

    • A Gazebo simulation of a TIAGo robot localizing in a previously mapped apartment
    • The robot has several onboard sensors and a manipulator which enable it to navigate and interact with the environment through simple manipulation tasks
    • The robot has modules for path planning, control, localization, sensing, and manipulation
    • The main mission is to navigate to a specific location to pick up an object, then transport it to a different target location
    • We made use of the available sensors and actuator through the software packages provided to accomplish the goal
    • The simulation uses Robot Operating System (ROS)
    • In the localization step, the robot spins around to gather as much information as possible from the enviroment using the sensors
    • Localization is done by using a particle filter. Random distribution of poses of the robot gets scattered across the map, then each one of them gets evaluated to find out how likely it is the true pose
    • The least likely ones get deleted, and the most likely ones are used to generate poses nearby in a reiterative manner to find out the correct pose
    • ARUCO markers were used to find the cube's pose. Once the robot can see the marker, it can target it's location with the manipulator
    • State Machine and Behaviour Tree were both used as models for the robots behaviour
    • The robot has the capability to recognize when it is kidnapped, and relocalizes when it occurs

    DECEPTIVE REVIEWS DETECTION THROUGH ONE-CLASS SVM RESEARCH

    • This three month work was part of a project related to the topic of Research Methodology and Scientific Writing
    • The work evaluates the performance of using One-Class Support Vector Machine (SVM) to detect deceptive reviews online
    • After performing extensive resarch in the field, previous efforts were found that used both genuine and deceptive reviews to train a multitude of Machine Learning (ML) algorithms
    • The objective of this project was to prove that only using the genuine reviews is sufficient to produce a machine learning classifier that performs better or as good as multi-class SVM without losing accuracy in classifications
    • Online reviews play an increasing role in business. In the digital world they have replaced the word of mouth. With all the benefits it brings, unfortunately fake reviews produced by bots falsify this data
    • The purpose of this work is to further facilitate the prevention of deceptive text from decreasing thecredibility of online reviews
    • This is done by developing a machine learning model to identify deceptive reviews that is simple enough to produce a better performance than a given existing solution without sacrificing accuracy
    • The project used quantitative techniques to classify review data-sets as deceptive or genuine. Once the ML algorithm was developed, an analytical method was used to quantify the performanceand accuracy. This was later compared to the approach of using the combination of genuine and deceptive reviews in order to confirm or to invalidate the stated hypothesis
    • The data-sets were gathered, cleaned up and merged into one table using R studio and Pandas. The data was then pre-processed before feeding it into the SVM algorithms
    • Extensive pre-processing was required through Natural Language Processing (NLP).This is essential to enable the computer to interpret the human language. The following five steps were performed to transform the review text into a form that can be fed into the algorithm:
      • Tokenization: To break a block of text into a list of words
      • Remove Stop words and Non-alpha text: Stop words such as (a, at, but, etc) and numbers are irrelevantand can create over-fitting issues. Therefore, they are removed from the text
      • Word Lemmatization: To group together all forms of a word so they can be analysed as a single item.For an example, change, changing, and changer
      • Word Vectorization: It is a general process of turning a collection of text documents into numericalfeature vectors. The method used is called TF-ID. The method summarizes how often a word appears,and down scales words that have a high frequency across all documents, which in return highlightsmore interesting words
      • Undersampling: To eliminate imbalance from the data-sets, undersampling removes samples from themajority class until all labels are equally represented

    ENTREPRENEURIAL BUSINESS VENTURE

    • The name of the startup venture is “Ebike Amp”. The startup is a new electric bike product that offers solutions for everyday commuting, or as a recreational/competitive sport
    • The bike has both pedaling and electric assist capabilities that would allow the driver to either get a comfortable and easy commute, or a hard workout if needed
    • Furthermore, the bike also can offer the driver a shield against the weather whether it’s raining, snowing, or just plain windy
    • The idea was generated due to a personal interest. I really enjoy biking and have been into it for many years
    • I started getting into biking as a hobby at the beginning, However, since I arrived in Sweden, it is my way of commuting as well. Multiple variables made biking here a lot more difficult than what I am used to, which in return made it unsustainable
    • I also have a great interest in automation, and an electronic bike was the perfect combination between the two interests, biking and automation
    • The concept of the ebike went through three iterations. The first iteration was a two-wheel ebike that looked very similar to current ebikes on the market, these forms of ebikes do not offer any form of protection against the environment
    • I wanted the ebike to easily offer the capability of transforming into a stationary bike, which currently no ebike manufacturers offer. Furthermore, I wanted to offer the capability of putting back power into the grid from pedaling while using the ebike in the stationary mode
    • With some research, I discovered that if you pedaled eight hours every day for 30 days (no weekends off), then doing the math, you'd generate about 16 kWh. A typical house in the U.S. uses about 1,000 kWh a month, which means with all that pedaling, would be able to save about 0.16% of your electric bill. With these results, putting power back to the grid is completely unsustainable and not worth going after
    • The second iteration was to create a bike that would use one wheel instead of two. However, that proved difficult to combine both stability and the possibility of pedaling while riding. While a one-wheel ebike would be more efficient and might be easier to weatherproof, it will also be very uncomfortable and harder to control. Furthermore, it’s a lot more complicated to stabilize
    • The third iteration was to put the driver inside the one wheel and provide means of assistance with balancing and turning. This iteration also made it possible to add weather protection without compromising the ebike dimensions, as it will be still enclosed within the wheel
    • The product is still in the concept phase, which means no prototype has been created nor tested
    • The Ebike Amp has innovative features that tackle multiple challenges when it comes to using bikes for commuting. The innovative solutions were developed for the following challenges:
      • The roads have too many hills: The Ebike Amp has an electric motor/generator to assist in pedaling. The user can have two options to choose how much assisting is required. The first option would be a torque multiplier, so as an example, the user can choose to multiply their torque input from pedaling by 1.5X. The second option is choosing the maximum torque applied by the user to the pedals. This option will make the pedal have a constant feel even if you are going on ramps or hills
      • Difficulty turning with one wheel: The Ebike Amp has electric motor/generators to assist in turning. The electric motors can control the two wheels to turn the bike. By slowing down the left wheel, and speeding up the right, the bike will turn left, and vice versa. The turn assist can also be used in regenerative breaking to transform breaking energy into charging the batteries. The turn assist frame can also swing upwards to reduce friction and increase efficiency when the bike is traveling in higher speeds
      • Un-versatile: the Ebike Amp can be also used as a stationary bike. If the user would like to train at home. The bike can be used as a stationary bike without any modifications. With just a press of a button, the motor used in assisting in pedaling can turn into a generator. That means if the bike is in stationary mode, the user can charge the ebike’s batteries. The turn assist frame would make sure the bike is standing up at all times
      • Not enough range in the batteries: The Ebike Amp has a large dome on the top that can host a large solar panel. A flexible solar panel can be installed on the dome to use solar energy to constantly charge the batteries. The Ebike Amp will also offer hot swappable batteries that can be changed in seconds
